SME restaurant operators often lack real-time financial intelligence to catch cost overruns and pricing gaps early — a pattern this framework refers to as "financial blindness," the lag between raw cost data and pricing decisions.
Design an AI-driven financial intelligence framework for SME restaurant operators — addressing invoice processing, menu pricing, and labor scheduling — with human accountability built into every financially consequential decision.
• Authored the framework's three core components: AI-OCR invoice ingestion, dynamic menu pricing recommendations, and predictive labor scheduling
• Grounded the design in independent research into SME restaurant technology adoption — POS vendor evaluation (Toast, Square, MarketMan), inventory/recipe-costing methodology, and payment-processing cost benchmarking
• Designed human-in-the-loop governance at every financially consequential decision point — no fully automated financial actions
Published as a preprint on ResearchGate/SSRN. Currently a concept and framework-design stage publication — implementation and measurable results are the next phase.

Global supply chains are increasingly vulnerable to adversarial entities — shell companies, sanctioned vendors, and financially distressed suppliers that evade traditional due diligence. Procurement teams at enterprises lack a unified, real-time risk signal that fuses financial health, geopolitical exposure, and entity relationship data.
Build an AI-powered multi-domain data fusion framework capable of detecting adversarial entities in procurement supply chains, with a live-validated, independently reproducible financial health signal module using SEC EDGAR XBRL data.
• Designed a four-layer ML architecture cross-referencing six data domains: corporate registries, beneficial ownership records, sanctions lists, financial transaction patterns, logistics data, and OSINT feeds
• Extended the framework with a SEC EDGAR XBRL financial health signal module
• Built the module to be independently reproducible and publicly documented
Published as a preprint on ResearchGate/SSRN. The SEC EDGAR XBRL financial health module is live-validated and independently reproducible — extending the original framework with active, ongoing technical development.
